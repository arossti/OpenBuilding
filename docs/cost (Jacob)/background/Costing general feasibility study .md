* **Investigate retail data acquisition**: Look into methods for gathering pricing data from major retailers like Lowe's or Home Depot, such as obtaining an API hook or scraping their websites.  
* **Evaluate data quality and variability**: Analyze the quality of various data sources, keeping in mind that retail pricing can fluctuate significantly depending on the region or the specific company.  
* **Develop updating protocols**: Research the best ways to keep the pricing data updated over time.  
* **Determine tool integration and display**: Figure out how the costs will be calculated (totaled and tallied) and how that information will be displayed to users within the tool.  
* **Conduct a general feasibility study**: Explore how costing, cost comparisons, and payback periods could fundamentally work within the application.
